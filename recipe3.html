Homemade Tomato Soup

  • 29 oz. can of whole San Marzano sytle tomatoes
  • 32 oz. carton of Kitchen Basics Vegetable Stock
  • 4-6 oz. half and half
  • 4 carrots - washed and unpeeled
  • 1 medium sized sweet onion - quartered
  • 1 medium red pepper - seeded and quartered
  • clove garlic - peeled
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1/2 tsp. sugar
  • Basil - 1-2 leaves whole
  • 4 tbsp. olive oil
  • Croutons or parmesan crisps for garnishing - optional

Instructions:

Brush 2 tbsp. olive oil on carrots, red pepper, onion, garlic and roast on a cookie sheet in 375 degree oven for 30 minutes or until vegetables are lightly browned and carrots are tender. In large saucepan, heat olive oil and add roasted vegetables, 16 oz. vegetable stock and san marzano tomatoes and puree vegetables with an immersion blender until smooth. Add salt, pepper, sugar, basil, an additional 8 oz. vegetable stock and 4 oz. half and half and stir until blended. Reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes. Garnish with croutons or parmesan crisps to serve.

About this Recipe:

With a few kitchen utensils, an immersion blender and a short list of items from your local grocery store you can be on your way to making an amazing homemade tomato soup. Food allergies and sensitivities are a growing concern for many people and cooking at home affords confidence in knowing exactly what goes into your food. You do not have to be a master chef to prepare healthy homemade soup, this recipe allows room for you to add, subtract, and swap out items with the end result perfectly tailored to your personal tastes. Suggested ingredient variations include 2% milk for less fat, coconut or almond milk for dairy-free. Recipe serves approximately 4-6 people.
